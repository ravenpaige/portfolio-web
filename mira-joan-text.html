Piece 01: Cages for the Innocents (2024)

View on artist site →
The Gate (Mesh Between)
[Meta‑Archetype Name]
Threshold • Justice • Contact
[e.g., Creation • Memory • Threshold]
Liminal: bodies split by a lattice; authority frontal, visitor occluded; communication forced through a grid.
[e.g., liminal / ascending / descending / radiant center]
To expose asymmetry at points of contact and test whether power becomes care or hardens into control.
[What it does in a psyche/system]
olive-drab #556B2F, ochre #D3B16A, bone-white #E6E1D6, charcoal #1A1A1A, steel-grey #8A8F93, muted blue #5F7E9A
[Dominant hues; symbolic valence]
Chain-link mesh: the Control Lattice made literal; vision and touch atomized. - Guard’s emptied eyes / shaved line: role eclipses personhood; procedure as mask. - Blurred crowd beyond panes: lives reduced to rumor; bureaucracy as fog. - Phone/hand at jaw: contact throttled to sanctioned channels. - Rectangular frames: rooms inside rooms; nested custody.
[Key motifs, forms, gestures]
The Gate is the human face of systems. Its right function is stewardship and clear boundaries; its shadow is domination disguised as order. The mesh turns relationship into units of control; consent turns it back into relationship. The question here is not “Who wins?” but “Who decides, on what grounds, and how can that yes be revoked?”
[2–5 sentences: essence, tension, shadow]
Stand, hands open. Inhale: I meet you at the mesh. Exhale: I do not become the mesh. Name one gate you hold (role, policy, habit). What is the smallest act of care you can perform without breaking your duty? Do it. If you’re the one at the net, ask for the boundary in plain words and the path to revoke.
[Short guidance: what to contemplate or try]
