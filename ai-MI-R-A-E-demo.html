MI-R-A-E: Strategic Rhetoric in the Age of AI

A modern framework for analyzing persuasive language ‚Äî whether human or machine-generated.

MI-R-A-E is a five-part structure for understanding how language operates in high-stakes communication. Unlike classical rhetoric‚Äôs Ethos/Pathos/Logos triangle, MI-R-A-E is directional ‚Äî built to follow the strategic arc of real-world messaging:

Motive + Intent ‚Üí Relation ‚Üí Audience ‚Üí Effect

üß© What the Components Mean

1. Motive + Intent

What‚Äôs the speaker trying to achieve beneath the surface? This includes emotional motives (reassurance, provocation), political or strategic goals (persuasion, distraction), or personal positioning (credibility, control).

2. Relation

How does the speaker frame their relationship to the subject and to the audience? This includes rhetorical stance, timing (kairos), authority claims (ethos), and narrative frame.

3. Audience

Who is the message calibrated for? How are audience beliefs, values, fears, or allegiances being activated? Are multiple audiences being targeted differently?

4. Effect

What is the actual or anticipated outcome? Consider emotional tone shifts, policy consequences, media amplification, and long-term narrative positioning.
